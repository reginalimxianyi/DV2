Carbon Dioxide Emission Rate Around the World

Based on the choropleth map which visualizes carbon dioxide emission rate of countries which is shown on the right, countries that are highest and lowest emitters of carbon dioxide can be discerned easily.

It is evident that many countries have high level of carbon dioxide emissions and proactive measures must be implemented to curb this issue.

Carbon Dioxide Emission by Fuel Type

The line chart on the left visualizes carbon dioxide emissions over the years based on different fuel types which are cement, coal, flaring, gas and oil.

This visualisation allows deeper understanding on carbon footprint in countries and its primary contributors. It can be observed that coal is the primary contributor of carbon dioxide emission in most countries.

Total Carbon Dioxide Emissions of Countries: A Fuel Type and Population Perspective

This chart visualises the total carbon dioxide emissions from two perspectives which are population and fuel type.

The proportion of a country's total carbon dioxide emission that each type of fuel contributes can be discerned from this chart. Besides, it can be observed that countries with larger populations typically have higher carbon dioxide emissions. However, this is not always the case.

Annual Carbon Dioxide Emission of Countries

The interactive bar chart on the left visualises the annual carbon dioxide from year 2000 to 2018 for Australia, Malaysia, Italy, Indonesia and India.

Carbon dioxide emission of Australia, India, Indonesia and Malaysia have increased over the years. On the other hand, the emission of carbon dioxide in Italy has decreased over the years.
